About Me

I'm a physician-scientist who believes the future of medicine lies at the intersection of clinical expertise and artificial intelligence.

After completing my orthopedic surgery training at Seoul National University Hospital and becoming board-certified, I recognized that the diagnostic patterns I developed in the operating room could be translated into AI systems that augment physician capabilities.

Now, as a postdoctoral fellow at Harvard Medical School working with Dr. Pranav Rajpurkar, I develop and validate AI models for medical imaging interpretation, clinical decision support, and healthcare workflow optimizationâ€”always grounded in real-world clinical needs.

My unique perspective as both a surgeon and AI researcher allows me to build systems that are not just technically sophisticated, but clinically meaningful.

Research Focus

My research aims to bridge the gap between AI innovation and clinical implementation, developing systems that work in the complexity of real healthcare environments.

Medical Vision AI

Developing multimodal AI systems for medical image interpretation, including knee MRI analysis with 3D spatial reasoning and radiology report generation with hallucination detection.

Clinical Decision Support

Building predictive models for surgical outcomes and patient-reported measures, enabling personalized treatment planning and evidence-based clinical decisions.

Healthcare AI Evaluation

Creating simulation frameworks like the Clinical Environment Simulator (CES) to assess AI performance under realistic hospital conditions beyond static benchmarks.
